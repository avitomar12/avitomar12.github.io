This pebble thought of dying without her.
He lives and breathes — just breathing.
I used to wonder, at what cost?
The cost of dreams they built from dust.
Dreams that never became real — 
And never will.

A moment of bright light,
In her eyes he’ll never see again.
He just breathes with these fragments,
Never turning, never moved,
A weight of love, a life removed.

Until someone picks me from the road,
Carries me back to my old abode,
And sets me near the vase to stay — 
A ghost too small to drift away.
