In the stillness of the dark,
Where the world quietly slips away,
You are left with your thoughts — 
Heavy, untold.
A heart full of desires,
And wounds that never seem to heal.

You walk a road unseen,
Longing for warmth that never stays,
For love that slipped through your grasp,
Regret carved into every step.

Walls rise from your pain,
Yet, still, in silence, you hope — 
Hope for touch, for care,
For something that might ease this weight,
Even if just for a while.

But the quiet grows deafening,
Tears fall without a sound.
You are both broken and strong — 
Trapped between a past
That won’t release its grip,
And a future
That feels too far to believe in.

And still, you breathe.
And that, for now, is enough.
