Sunset

The sunset is beautiful — 
its redness deep, its intensity full.
Every time I witness such beauty,
I can’t look away.
Tears begin to fall.
Not quite crying — 
just something soft breaking open inside me.
I feel a sadness,
not from what is there,
but from what I’ve forgotten.
So many moments lost,
too few glances at nature
held long enough to remember.
Maybe this is how life was meant to be — 
Nature, or something more,
perhaps a hymn,
or just human emotion,
nudging me gently:
“Look again, feel deeper.”
And yet, something resists.
A strange stillness in me
tries to stop these words
from flowing free.
But I write anyway, through fading light — 
for even sunsets deserve to be written right.
