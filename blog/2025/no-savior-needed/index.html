Silence of the grass field,
Thick air that grips, won’t let me yield.
Dust in the sky makes me blind,
I close my eyes, hold my breath, unwind.
Your face appears — then I push it away,
No savior needed in this void I’ve made.

The darkness wraps me, cold and tight,
I’ll carve my path alone through the night.
The snow falls heavy, a frozen grave,
Biting winds howl, but I stay brave.
Each step sinks deep, the cold cuts through,
This desolate world needs no help from you.

The rain pours down, a deafening roar,
Drenched to the bone, I seek no more.
Each drop whispers I’m still alive,
Through storm and shadow, alone I’ll thrive.
